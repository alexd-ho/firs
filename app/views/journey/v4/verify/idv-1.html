Assign the Senior Responsible Officer

Before you start, check if:

  • you are the Senior Responsible Officer (SRO)
  • someone else is the SRO
{% from 'govuk/components/details/macro.njk' import govukDetails %} {{ govukDetails({ summaryText: 'What is a Senior Responsible Officer?', text: 'A Senior Responsible Officer (SRO) is a person who is responsible for ensuring compliance with the Foreign Influence Registration Scheme (FIRS) within an entity. The SRO is typically a senior executive within the entity who has the authority to make decisions about FIRS and is the single point of contact for the FIRS team. ' }) }}

If you are the SRO

You must verify your identity and provide your person details.

If someone else is the SRO

  1. Ask them to create their own Foreign Influence Registration Scheme (FIRS) account.
  2. Ask them for their Foreign Influence Registration Scheme account number. They can find this on their account page when they sign in.
  3. Continue this section and enter their FIRS account number.
  4. You must review the account information they have provided and confirm you want to assign them as the SRO.
  5. We will send them an email asking them to confirm they are the SRO for your entity. They will need to verify their identity and provide their personal details.
