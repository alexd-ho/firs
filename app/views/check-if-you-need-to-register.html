Check if you need to register

You must register on FIRS if you are eligible.

You're eligible if you agree to carry out an activity on behalf of a foreign government, a sovereign, or head of state that can influence:

  • an election or referendum in the UK
  • a decision made by a Minister or Government department
  • how a UK registered political party operates
  • a Member of the House of Commons, House of Lords, Northern Ireland Assembly, Scottish Parliament or Senedd Cymru

An activity can be any:

  • private communication such as an email, letter or meeting
  • public communication such as an article or publication
  • offer of money, goods or services to an individual or entity in the UK

Read more about when you must register on FIRS.

When you do not need to register

You may not need to register if:

  • the UK Government are a part of, or have their given authority to the activity
  • you are a foreign government, sovereign, or head of state
  • you are a diplomat or a spouse, partner or family member of a diplomat carrying out activities that are reasonably necessary for the functioning of a diplomatic mission
  • you are a recognised news publisher
  • you are a lawyer or solicitor carrying out legal activity as part of your work

Read more about when you do not need to register on FIRS.
