Check if you need to register

You must register on FIRS if you meet these 2 conditions.

Condition 1

You arrange or carry out an activity on behalf of a:

  • foreign government or local authority
  • Sovereign
  • Head of State
  • listed person or entity

An arrangement is any formal or informal agreement.

An activity can be any:

  • private communication such as an email, letter or meeting
  • public communication such as an article or publication
  • offer of money, goods or services to an individual or entity in the UK
  • specific listed activity

Condition 2

The arrangement or activity can influence:

  • an election or referendum in the UK
  • a decision made by a Minister or Government department
  • how a UK registered political party operates
  • a Member of the House of Commons, House of Lords, Northern Ireland Assembly, Scottish Parliament or Senedd Cymru

Deadline to register

In most cases, you must register a new arrangement within 28 days, starting from the day the arrangement is made.

You can carry out activities immediately but you must register the arrangement within 28 days.

However, there are different rules for certain arrangements and activities.

For example, you'll need to register a new arrangement within 10 days if:

  • you arrange or carry out an activity on behalf of a listed person or entity
  • you arrange or carry out a specific listed activity

When you may not need to register

You may not need to register if:

  • the UK Government is a party to your arrangement
  • you are a foreign government or local authority, Sovereign, or Head of State
  • you are a diplomat or a spouse, partner or family member of a diplomat
  • you are carrying out activities that are reasonably necessary for the functioning of a diplomatic mission
  • you are a recognised news publisher
  • you are a lawyer or solicitor carrying out legal activity
